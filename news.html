News & notices

Official statements, programme updates and government notices from the Citizenship Office and Commission.

2025 · Statement

Issuance of Vanuatu diplomatic passports marketed overseas

Following coverage in the Daily Post (Issue No. 7209) and on social media, the Commission clarifies that it processes citizenship applications and issues Citizenship Certificates — it does not issue passports. Green passports are issued by Immigration & Passport Services; diplomatic passports by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

2025 · Notice

Vanuatu FIU due-diligence checks

As part of an ongoing review of due-diligence services for the DSP, CIIP and VCP programmes, the Vanuatu Financial Intelligence Unit issued a new process to better manage and safeguard its due-diligence procedures.

December 2024 · Press release

Strengthening the programme with enhanced due diligence and investment verification

The Commission announced significant enhancements to the citizenship programme — "a new era in the nation's commitment to international best practices and economic development."

Programme

Real Estate Option Programme (REO)

The REO programme commenced 23 July 2021. Applicants must invest in property in a Vanuatu Government Approved Project. Four approved projects: Pacific Springs, Narpow Point Coral Bay and FPF Rainbow City (Efate), and Milai (Espiritu Santo).

View REO programme
Enforcement

Revocations

The current Commission has revoked almost 30 citizenships that were granted contrary to the Citizenship Act [Cap 112] and the Constitution, and continues to require that all applicants apply through the proper process.

Application forms updated

The Vanuatu citizenship application forms have been systematically renumbered; the amended forms are available on the website.

Update

CIIP suspension lifted

The Government lifted the suspension of the Capital Investment Immigration Plan (CIIP) as of 21 March 2016. The Commission approved three agents to operate the Plan in accordance with Order 93 of 2023.

About the CIIP
Legislation

Amendments to the Citizenship Act [Cap 112]

The Act has been amended several times since 2013, most recently in 2021. Vanuatu now recognises dual citizenship, and the application and certificate fees have been revised.

Legislative framework
Notice

Access to the Citizenship Office (PMO compound)

Visitors to the Office (within the Prime Minister's Office compound) must dress appropriately, hold prior appointments, observe parking and lift restrictions, and refrain from smoking; access is denied to those who do not comply.

Notice

Information for agents & applicants

The Government, through its Citizenship Commission and Office, issued information for designated agents and persons interested in its citizenship programmes.

Directive

Enforcement of government-prescribed fees

All DSP and VCP agents must adhere to government-stipulated pricing, as set out in Regulations Order 33 of 24 April 2019 (DSP) and Order 34 of 24 April 2019 (VCP).

Oath-taking on behalf of the Commission

23 July 2021

Naika Holdings appointed Master Agent of the REO Programme

The Commission appointed Naika Holdings Limited as the exclusive master agent of the Citizenship (Real Estate Option) Programme, effective 23 July 2021.

2021 · Programme

Commencement of the REO programme

The Real Estate Option Programme commenced under Section 13F of the Citizenship Act and the Citizenship (Real Estate Option Programme) Regulation Order No. 93 of 2021.
